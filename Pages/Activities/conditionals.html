Conditional Sentences

Grammar Overview: Conditional Sentences

Conditional sentences consist of two halves, known as clauses[cite: 437]:

  • The "if" part (if clause): Sets the condition[cite: 438].
  • The other part (main clause): Uses words like can, will, may, might, could, or would to show the result[cite: 439].

Zero Conditional (100%)

Used for general truths and scientific facts.

If + Present Simple, Present Simple

1st Conditional (75%)

Used for real possibilities in the present or future.

If + Present Simple, Will + Verb

2nd Conditional (25%)

Used for unreal or hypothetical situations[cite: 448, 449].

If + Past Simple, Would + Verb

3rd Conditional (0%)

Used for past regrets or things that did not happen[cite: 453, 454, 456].

If + Past Perfect, Would have + P.P.

Tip: If the "if" clause comes first, use a comma. If it comes second, no comma is needed (e.g., "Water boils if you heat it")[cite: 467, 468, 469].
