Conditional Sentences

Grammar Overview: Conditional Sentences

Conditional sentences consist of two halves, known as clauses:

  • The "if" part (if clause): Sets the condition.
  • The other part (main clause): Uses words like can, will, may, might, could, or would to show the result.

Zero Conditional (100%)

General truths and facts.

If + Pres., Pres.

1st Conditional (75%)

Real possibilities.

If + Pres., Will + V

2nd Conditional (25%)

Unreal/Hypothetical.

If + Past, Would + V

3rd Conditional (0%)

Past regrets.

If + P.Perf, Would have + PP

Tip: If the "if" clause comes first, use a comma.
